With the spread between 7/1 ARMs and 30-year fixed rates now under 20 basis points, a 7/1 ARM only makes sense in 2026 if you’re certain you’ll sell or refinance within five years — not seven. After year seven, your rate can adjust up to 2% annually, capped at 5% over the initial rate, which could push payments from ~$1,800 to over $2,300 on a $300k loan. According to our break-even calculator at QFINHUB, you’d need to save at least $1,200 in total interest during the fixed period just to cover the risk of that first adjustment.
