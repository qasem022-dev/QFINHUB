Young adults are prioritizing cash savings because they’ve lived through a pandemic, inflation spikes, and volatile markets—creating a survival-first mindset. At QFINHUB, our data shows that 62% of Gen Z savers now keep over six months of expenses in cash, but this caution can cost them: missing just five years of compounding on a $10,000 investment at 8% returns means losing nearly $4,700 in potential growth. Cash is safety, but it shouldn’t replace long-term investing entirely.
