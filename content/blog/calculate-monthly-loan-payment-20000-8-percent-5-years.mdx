## The Exact Math Behind Your Monthly Payment

If you borrow $20,000 at an 8% annual interest rate for 5 years, your monthly payment comes to **$405.53**.

But how do you get that number? Let's break it down.

### The Amortization Formula

The standard formula for a fixed-rate loan is:

```
M = P × [ r(1+r)^n ] / [ (1+r)^n − 1 ]
```

Where:
- **M** = monthly payment
- **P** = principal ($20,000)
- **r** = monthly interest rate (8% ÷ 12 = 0.006667)
- **n** = total number of payments (5 years × 12 = 60)

### Plugging In the Numbers

```
P = 20,000
r = 0.08 / 12 = 0.006667
n = 5 × 12 = 60

(1+r)^n = (1.006667)^60 = 1.4898

M = 20,000 × [0.006667 × 1.4898] / [1.4898 − 1]
M = 20,000 × 0.009932 / 0.4898
M = 20,000 × 0.02028
M = $405.53
```
